Summary

A 6-month multifaceted project in collaboration with Waag Future Lab's Hollandse Luchten, dedicated to raising awareness about air pollution in the Netherlands. Resulted in a concept that includes a fully-featured air-focused fitness tracking app and a community-based "marathon for awareness" initiative.

Design challenge

Though a highly relevant topic, poor air quality across the Netherlands remains underdiscussed. Hollandse Luchten has launched a citizen awareness initiative, but most of its participants are elderly citizens, and the project lacks engagement from other demographics.

After some research and ideation, health-conscious sport-oriented people were chosen as the target demographic for the project, which includes youth as well as active parents concerned about the environment's impact on their children's health.

Research and Ideation

  • Analyzed client goals, their existing resources, and other similar initiatives to identify gaps in usability and engagement.
  • Used brainstorming (Braindumping, Lotus Blossom) to generate multiple design concepts.
  • Narrowed ideas down to a combined "fitness app + awareness marathon" concept focused on creating a sense of community.

Prototyping and Design

  • Designed lo-fi to hi-fi app prototypes in Figma, including air quality maps, run tracking, a community/event feed, and event registration.
  • Created a design system (components, icons, grid) to ensure visual consistency.
  • Designed branding materials, mascot, infographics, posters, and sustainable merchandise to support the campaign.

Project management

  • Worked in Scrum (with evolving roles and retrospectives), dynamically adjusting the workflow based on relevant tasks and user stories.
  • Conducted regular client presentations and integrated feedback from both testers and stakeholders.
  • Presented the results at a university expo with a hi-fi prototype, sustainable merchandise samples, and a live demo setup.
